### Q: Do personal projects need a commercial license?
A: No. Personal learning, research, and non-commercial use can freely use the AGPL-3.0 version.

### Q: Do open source projects need a commercial license?
A: No. Open source projects can freely use the AGPL-3.0 version.

### Q: I want to provide SaaS services without open-sourcing my code. What should I do?
A: You need to purchase a commercial license. According to AGPL-3.0, network services must be open-sourced; a commercial license exempts this requirement.

### Q: After purchasing a commercial license, do future version updates require additional payment?
A: This depends on your chosen license model (annual subscription or perpetual license), which will be specified in the contract.

### Q: Can I try it first before deciding to purchase a commercial license?
A: Yes. You can first use the AGPL-3.0 version for evaluation, then contact for commercial licensing after confirming suitability.
